# Babcock University Student Portal (Pulse) AI Knowledge Base
**Version:** 1.1.0
**Target Audience:** Pulse AI Support Chatbot & Course Advisor

This document contains the official operating guidelines, portal workflows, academic regulations, and common student troubleshooting FAQs for Babcock University.

---

## 1. The 4-Phase Master Enrollment Flow
Every student must complete these four phases in strict sequence to complete registration for each semester.

### Phase 1: Discovery & Academic Preparation
* **Goal:** Strategic situational awareness and prerequisite review.
* **Steps:**
  1. Direct access to the **Notification Center** via the Dashboard to locate registration start/end announcements.
  2. Review past academic history and GPA in **Academic Details** -> **Semester Results**.
  3. Identify any **Carry Over Courses** or **Repeated Courses** that need immediate re-registration.

### Phase 2: Course Registration & Validation
* **Goal:** Academic compliance and slot reservation.
* **Rules & Steps:**
  1. Open the **Registration Module** -> **Register Courses**.
  2. Select the designated **Class Group** for the current semester.
  3. Add both **Current Semester Courses** and any **Carry Over Courses**.
  4. Ensure the total load does **not exceed 23 credit units** (standard limit is 21 units, up to 23 units with departmental overload approval).
  5. Review the **Summary of Selected Units** and submit.
  6. **Print the Course Form** for physical signature and record-keeping.

### Phase 3: Financial Clearance & Ancillary Services
* **Goal:** Transaction completion and student welfare onboarding.
* **Rules & Steps:**
  1. Navigate to the **Finance Module** -> **School Fees**.
  2. Select **Residence** status (Hall of Residence or Off-Campus approval).
  3. Choose a **Meal Plan** (2 meals/day, 3 meals/day, or pay-as-you-go).
  4. Select a preferred **Worship Center** on campus.
  5. Perform checkout. Students can select **Partial Payment** (minimum 60% of fees to clear for exams) or **Full Payment**.
  6. Complete the transaction via the **Payment Gateway**.
  7. Confirm success and click **Show Receipt** to print/save the receipt.

### Phase 4: Administrative Maintenance
* **Goal:** Data hygiene and institutional records update.
* **Steps:**
  1. Open **My Profile**.
  2. Verify contact details and perform a mandatory update to **Next of Kin** details.

## 2. Comprehensive Student FAQs (Portal, Academic & Campus Life)

### Domain A: Financials & Payment Troubleshooting
#### Q1: My school fees payment failed, what should I do?
1. **Check your bank statement or transaction history** first. Do not make a double payment! Confirm if the amount was actually debited.
2. **Wait 2-3 hours.** Sometimes there is a processing lag between the payment gateway and the portal updating.
3. If the funds were debited but the portal still says "Payment Failed" after 3 hours, do not panic. Take a screenshot of the debit alert or your bank receipt and open a support ticket under **Support Tickets** or visit the **Bursary Unit** (Finance Office) with your receipt.

#### Q2: Can I pay my school fees in installments (Partial Payment)?
* **Yes!** Babcock University allows a minimum **60% partial payment** of your school fees to clear you for course registration and the first half of the semester.
* The remaining **40% balance** must be fully paid and cleared before you are permitted to sit for the final semester examinations.

### Domain B: Course Registration & Academic Advising
#### Q3: I cannot add more courses, it says "Credit Unit Limit Exceeded".
* The standard maximum limit is **21 credit units** per semester.
* You can register up to **23 credit units** only if you have departmental overload approval from your Head of Department (HOD) or Dean (typically reserved for final-year students clearing graduation requirements). If you exceed the limit, you must drop an elective or visit your HOD for clearance.

#### Q4: How do I handle a carry-over course?
* You **must prioritize and register all carry-over courses first** during Phase 2 before adding your normal current-semester courses.
* Note that you cannot register for a course if you haven't passed its official prerequisite. If you have carry-overs, they must be cleared as early as possible to prevent graduation delays.

#### Q5: Can I request a prerequisite waiver?
* Prerequisite rules are strictly enforced by the portal. In rare, exceptional circumstances (e.g., final-year graduation clearance), a waiver can be approved manually by your HOD. You must submit a physical waiver request form to your department.

### Domain C: Academic Grading & Degree Classification
#### Q6: What is the Babcock University official grading scale?
The university operates on a **5-point grading system**:
* **A (Excellent):** 80% – 100% (5.0 Grade Points)
* **B (Very Good):** 60% – 79% (4.0 Grade Points)
* **C (Good):** 50% – 59% (3.0 Grade Points)
* **D (Satisfactory):** 45% – 49% (2.0 Grade Points)
* **E (Pass):** 40% – 44% (1.0 Grade Point)
* **F (Fail):** Below 40% (0.0 Grade Points)

#### Q7: How are degrees classified at graduation?
Your final Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) determines your degree class:
* **First Class Honours:** 4.50 – 5.00 CGPA
* **Second Class Honours (Upper Division):** 3.50 – 4.49 CGPA
* **Second Class Honours (Lower Division):** 2.50 – 3.49 CGPA
* **Third Class:** 2.00 – 2.49 CGPA

#### Q8: What are the consequences of missing classes?
* **80% Class Attendance Rule:** Students must attend at least 80% of all lectures in a course to be eligible to sit for its examination.
* **Failure due to Absence (FA):** If your attendance falls below 80%, you receive an automatic **"FA"** grade (equivalent to an F/0.0 GP).
* **3-Week Absence Rule:** Missing classes consecutively for **three weeks or more** during the semester results in an automatic **"F"** grade.

### Domain D: Hostel Accommodation & Student Welfare
#### Q9: Can I change my hall of residence after payment?
* Once you select a hall of residence and complete payment, your room allocation is locked in the system.
* To request a hall transfer, you must visit the **Student Affairs Division** at the Student Affairs building. Room changes are subject to space availability and manual administrative approval.

#### Q10: What are the accommodation categories available on campus?
* **Premium (Classic):** Fewer students per room, modern amenities, en-suite bathrooms.
* **Regular:** Standard student occupancy (4-6 students), shared bathroom facilities.
* **Basic:** Higher density student occupancy, standard facilities.

#### Q11: What are the visiting hours and opposite-sex rules in hostels?
* **No Opposite-Sex Hostel Room Access:** Male students are strictly banned from entering female hostels, and female students are banned from male hostels. There is a zero-tolerance policy.
* **Visiting Hours:** Common lounges are open for same-sex visits from **4:00 PM to 7:00 PM** on weekdays, and **10:00 AM to 6:00 PM** on Sundays.
* **Banned Items:** High-voltage or cooking appliances (boiling rings, hot plates, electric kettles, microwaves, toasters) are strictly prohibited inside rooms.

### Domain E: Spiritual Life, Curfew & Campus Dress Code
#### Q12: What happens if I miss mandatory Chapel services?
* Attendance at Wednesday Chapel, Saturday Sabbath Services, and departmental spiritual seminars is **compulsory** for all students.
* Absences are monitored and recorded. Accumulating unexcused absences leads to character disciplinary points, suspension, or withholding of portal/hostel privileges. Medical excuses must be officially cleared by BUTH.

#### Q13: What is the campus curfew?
* The campus gates and all hostels lock strictly at **10:00 PM** daily. All students must be inside their respective halls of residence before this time. 

#### Q14: What is the official Babcock University dress code?
Babcock University enforces a strict corporate dress code on weekdays (8:00 AM to 5:00 PM) and at all official gatherings:
* **For Male Students:** Clean low-cut hair only. Beards, dreadlocks, bushy or voluminous hair are strictly prohibited. Collared shirts must be tucked in with a tie, corporate trousers, and formal shoes.
* **For Female Students:** Corporate skirts or dresses reaching at least **3 inches below the knee**. Sleeveless tops, body-hugging (Lycra) materials, and plunging necklines are completely prohibited.
* **General Prohibitions:**
  * **No Denim/Jeans:** All denim and jean-like materials are strictly banned on campus at all times.
  * **Traditional/Casual Limits:** Bùbù and Dànsíkí outfits, joggers, and sportswear are banned during academic/official hours.
  * **Accessories:** Headscarves worn over corporate wear are prohibited. Nose rings, tongue rings, body piercings, and necklaces are strictly prohibited.
  * **Hair-Passes:** All previously issued "hair-passes" or grooming waivers are completely **invalid, null, and void**.

## 3. General Campus Rules & Code of Conduct
* **Corporate Decency:** The university maintains a zero-tolerance policy for suggestive, revealing, or tight-fitting clothing to protect the faith-based campus environment.
* **Curfew Lockout:** Any student found outside their hostel after 10:00 PM without an official late-pass faces immediate disciplinary panel action.
* **Chapel Records:** Your spiritual attendance record is directly tied to your end-of-semester administrative clearance.
